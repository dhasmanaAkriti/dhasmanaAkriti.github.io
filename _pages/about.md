Hi! I am Akriti Dhasmana. I am an incoming PhD student in the Computer Science Department of University of Colorado Boulder. I will be advised by [Dr. Maria Antoniak](https://maria-antoniak.github.io/) and conducting researchwork as a part of the [Culture, Language and Systems Lab](https://maria-antoniak.github.io/antoniak-lab). Before joining CU Boulder, I completed my Master's in Computer Science and Engineering at the University of Notre Dame where I was advised by [Dr. David Chiang](https://academicweb.nd.edu/~dchiang/). 
As a part of the NLP Lab at Notre Dame, I worked on Automated Speech Recognition for low-resource Indic dialects and language varities.

My current research interests lie in cultural analytics, AI assisted fiction writing, low-resource NLP and automated speech recognition. Broadly, my research interests are as follows:
- **Cultural Analytics & Narrative Understanding**: Advancing computational tools for imroving narrative understanding and cultural analytics for texts written in low-resource languages.
- **Low Resource NLP**: Improving the scope, accessibility and quality of various NLP tools for low-resource linguistic communities.
In the future, I hope to continue improving support for low-resource Indic languages. 
- **AI Assisted Fiction Writing**: Developing tools for writers who work in multilingual and multi-modal creative spaces.
